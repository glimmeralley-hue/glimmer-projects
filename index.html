Ancient Beginnings

Long before electricity, humanity found clever ways to stay cool.

The earliest records of fans date back to 3000 BC. Ancient Egyptians used giant lotus leaves or peacock feathers. In Japan and China, the folding fan became a staple of both fashion and ceremony.

The Industrial Shift

From hand-powered to belt-driven machinery.

In the 1800s, factories used large, belt-driven fans powered by steam engines to keep machines from overheating.

1882: A Spark of Genius

The First Design

Schuyler Wheeler invented the two-blade electric fan.

Home Adoption

By the 1920s, fans were common in households worldwide.

Safety First

Protective cages were added as fans moved into the home.

Modern Mastery

The Dyson "Air Multiplier" changed everything in 2009.

Modern fans are smart, bladeless, and even purify the air we breathe using HEPA filters and Wi-Fi connectivity.

The Silent Language

Secret Signals

In the 18th century, fans were used to send secret romantic codes in ballrooms.

Performance

Fans remain essential in traditional dances from Spain to South Korea.
